Privacy Policy

MedVault — Offline Pill Reminder & Medicine Tracker

Last Updated: March 2026

Thank you for choosing MedVault. Your privacy is extremely important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how MedVault handles the information you enter and how we protect it.

1. Information We Collect

MedVault is designed as a privacy-first offline application.

By default, the app does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data with external servers. All information you enter into the app is stored locally on your device only.

Examples of data you may store in the app include (but are not limited to):

  • Medication names, dose instructions, and schedules
  • Medication history logs (taken/missed/skipped)
  • Allergies and emergency contact information
  • Profile data for patients you manage (name, date of birth, relation)
  • Photos or images you attach to medication or profiles

None of this information leaves your device unless you explicitly export or share it.

2. No Internet Data Collection

MedVault does not automatically send any information over the internet. We do not collect:

  • Personal identifiers (name, email) unless you input them into the app
  • Health records to our servers
  • Location data, contacts, or device identifiers
  • Behavioral analytics or usage telemetry (default)

3. Local Data Storage

All app data is stored locally on your device using Android storage mechanisms. This means:

  • Your data remains under your control.
  • No cloud database stores your health information by default.
  • The developer does not have access to your data unless you explicitly export and share backup files.

4. Backup & Export

MedVault may provide options to create backups or export data. Key points:

  • Backups are created only when you choose to export or back up your data.
  • Backups can be stored locally on your device or a storage location you select.
  • We recommend encrypting backup files with a passphrase when that option is available.
  • If you upload backups to cloud storage, that upload is initiated and controlled by you.

5. Permissions

MedVault requests the minimum set of permissions required for core functionality. These may include:

  • Notifications: Required to deliver medication reminders and alerts.
  • Storage access (optional): Only if you manually export or restore backup files via the device storage or SAF (Storage Access Framework).

The app will explain why a permission is requested before it is asked for. You may deny optional permissions and the app will continue to function in local-only mode.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal health data with third parties. Any sharing of data (for example, exporting a CSV or sending a PDF) is initiated and controlled by you.

7. Children’s Privacy

We do not knowingly collect or transmit personal information from children. Since MedVault operates offline, no child data is transmitted to the developer.

8. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect the data stored on your device. Recommended user practices include:

  • Use a screen lock (PIN, pattern, or biometrics)
  • Enable full-disk encryption on your device
  • Store backups in a secure location and use a passphrase for exported backups

Note: Because data is stored locally, if an attacker gains access to your unlocked device, they may be able to view app data.

9. Third-Party Services

MedVault does not integrate third-party advertising networks or analytics services by default. If any third-party integration is added in the future (for optional features such as cloud backup), it will be explicitly documented and configured as an opt-in feature.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page will reflect the most recent change. Substantive changes will be communicated in-app where feasible.
